Welcome to Crypt Sweepers

Flip tiles to explore the dungeon. Each tile hides an enemy, trap, treasure, or empty space. Numbers on revealed tiles hint at nearby threats — like Minesweeper.

Tap an enemy to fight. You strike, then they strike. Kill in one hit to skip their counter. Fast enemies ambush on reveal — watch out.

Reach the exit 🚪 on each floor to descend deeper. Death ends the run, but your progress carries over.

💡 Mana and abilities don't appear until you unlock them. Visit your hero's upgrade tree between runs to spend earned XP — that's where your power comes from.

🎯 Your first few runs will be tough. That's intentional — each run you'll go a little deeper.

📖 How to Play

Start here. The essentials are up top. Open a section below when you want detail — you don’t need it all on your first run.

💡 Hold (long-press) any tile, item, or enemy to pop up an info card about it — monsters show stats, items describe what they do, and tiles explain their effects.

🗺 Flip the dungeon

Crypt Sweepers is a roguelike tile-flipper: each floor is a hidden grid. You start on one revealed tile and expand by flipping orthogonally adjacent hidden tiles (north, south, east, west — not diagonals). Only tiles marked as reachable can be flipped.

Threat clues — small numbers on revealed tiles (where enabled) sum the threat level of living enemies and certain hazards in the four orthogonal directions, similar to Minesweeper. Use them to guess where danger is clustered before you commit.

📚 More depth

Optional reading — hazards, terrain, and meta.

🚩 Pressure on the floor
🚩
War flag — a tile may spawn that buffs every living enemy’s HP and damage until you destroy it. Often worth bee-lining before the floor snowballs.
🏹
Goblin Archer — appears early and sometimes on later floors. It starts revealed and pesters you each turn until dealt with — close in, or snipe with spells/actives.
💰
Treasure Goblin — rarely appears, already revealed, with a turn countdown. Path to him before it hits zero to earn a rare trinket; otherwise he escapes.
🕳 Sub-floors & terrain

Sub-floors — special entrances lead to a floor inside the floor (boss vaults, dens, treasure rooms, shrines, tunnels, and more), each with its own small grid.

Holes & rubble — open pits are always visible and impassable; rubble blocks paths until revealed. Path around them.

🎒 Backpack & item rarity

The backpack holds up to 9 items. When full, new finds ask you to swap or drop something.

⬜ Common — potions, tools, consumables
🔷 Rare — strong passive trinkets
🟣 Epic — high-impact relics with real tradeoffs
🟡 Legendary — run-defining relics (floor 10+)
Read trinket text — many trigger on equip or in specific situations.
⚔️ Equipment

Tap your hero portrait (bottom-left of the HUD) to open the equipment screen. Three dedicated slots hold your gear:

⚔️
Weapon — raises your damage range.
🧥
Breastplate — adds base armor that absorbs hits.
🛡️
Offhand — shields, orbs, and secondary weapons with mixed stat bonuses.

Gear is found in chests, as drops, and in shops. When you pick up an item that can go in a slot, a compare card appears showing the stat change — green means improvement. You can also drag gear from your backpack directly into a slot.

💡 Unequipped gear stays in your backpack. If your backpack is full and you find a new piece, you'll be asked to swap or drop.
🔨 Forge

Open the Forge from the main menu (next to Heroes) to tune your equipped gear between runs. You spend persistent gold and scrap here — not run gold from your current dungeon.

⬆️
Upgrade — select an equipped piece and forge up to 3 tiers. Each success rolls a random stat boost; higher tiers cost more and can fail.
⚙️
Disassemble — destroy a piece you are not wearing to gain scrap. Higher tiers yield more.
🔧
Detriment reduction — after all 3 upgrades, negative stats on a piece (Brittle Guard, Barbed Gear, Mana Drain) can be softened for an extra gold + scrap cost.

Scrap also comes from trashing gear mid-run from your backpack (smaller yield). Plan which pieces to keep, upgrade, or break down as your build takes shape.

💡 The in-dungeon Sanctuary Forge (trinket tile) is different — it merges two trinkets into a forged relic, not gear upgrades.
⚒️ Sanctuary Forge — Trinket Recipes

Combine two trinkets at the Sanctuary Forge tile to create a powerful merged relic. Both ingredients are consumed. Hold any trinket in your backpack to see if it has a forge combination.

🪬 Trinkets

Trinkets are passive items that apply their effect automatically — no activation required. They sit in your backpack and work as long as they're carried.

🔷
Rare trinkets — strong run-shaping passives. Found in chests, rare drops, and the shop.
🟡
Legendary trinkets — run-defining relics with powerful or unusual effects. Very rare.

Hold (long-press) any trinket in your backpack to read its full effect. Some trigger on specific events (on kill, on level up, on low HP), others modify a stat permanently for the run.

🔭
Spyglass — activate from the backpack, then tap a hidden tile. A category hint (⚔️ enemy, 🕸️ trap, 🪙 loot, etc.) stays on that tile until you flip it.
💡 Trinkets are not equipped in gear slots — they take up backpack space. Managing backpack inventory becomes a core decision as you go deeper.
🏠 Between runs (meta)
🗡️
Heroes — unlock heroes; each has unique tools and Hero Passives (see hero carousel).
⚡
Class XP tree — spend XP on that hero’s upgrades (including active abilities as you unlock tiers). Abilities and the mana bar don’t appear in-run until unlocked here.
📜
Passive Upgrades — account-wide bonuses (HP, damage, economy) bought with persistent gold — not the same as per-hero passives.
🛒
Gold Shop — spend run gold on consumables and unlocks for the next run.

💡 Quick tips
📊 Use threat numbers before you flip into a cluster of foes.
🚩 Don’t ignore a war flag for too long if enemies are getting scary.
🏹 Prioritize a Goblin Archer if it’s shredding you from range.
🔑 Golden keys — full floor clears can earn keys for magic chests in sanctuaries.
🪙 Retreat — better to escape with partial gold than die with everything (see in-game rules).

Numbers, spawn rates, and unlock flow are under active balance. If something feels off after a patch, check here again — it may have changed.
